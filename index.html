After publishing useful advice on good practices in removing unwanted hair, HPS magazine asked you, our readers, about the pros & cons of the most common hair removal methods - shaving, waxing and laser epilation. 1430 of you fulfilled the HPS questionnaire. First of all, let’s find out why popular removal methods don't work.

Shaving Causes Cuts, Waxing Hurts, Laser Epilation Is Too Expensive

1. SHAVING

Ineffective: Fast and cheap, but can cause skin irritations, undergrowth/hard hair growth. Short-term smoothness.

79% of those who have used a razor to remove unwanted hair admit they would never use this method again. The most common reasons for that are: new hair grows faster and thicker (83% of the respondents reported it); experiencing issues with cuts or scrapes (79%). More than half of the “shavers” (55%) claim that after shaving the hair grows inside the skin and leaves a black mark on the outside.

overall rate*: 1.9/10 (1430 respondents)

2. WAXING

Ineffective: Removes hair at the root, but requires time and effort; can also result in ingrown hairs. Painful procedure.

Certainly, the physical ripping of the hair out of the follicle is not an enjoyable sensation - 93% state waxing procedures (at-home or at beauty salons) are painful and time-taking. Most of the respondents notice that their hair growth becomes less dense but experience bumps and ingrown hair after the method - 78%. 73% of our “waxer” readers would prefer to use a painless method if it is safe and long-lasting.

overall rate*: 2.3/10 (1430 respondents)

3. LASER EPILATION

Ineffective: Long-lasting and removes even very short hairs, but risky - unsuitable for sensitive skin. Expensive hair removal method.

Laser epilation requires numerous visits to a dermatologist for best results. HPS research shows that 89% of those who have undergone time-consuming procedures complain that they have paid out a lot of money and are not satisfied with the results. Some of the respondents (57%) experienced irritation and redness in the affected areas, others (49%) - scars and even changes in skin colour.

overall rate*: 2.1/10 (1430 respondents)

*The overall rate is based on 3 categories: price-performance ratio, side effects occurrence and users’ experience.
